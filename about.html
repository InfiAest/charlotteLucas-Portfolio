About me.

I'm Charlotte. A front-end developer from the UK but based in Bergen, Norway.

After studying CGI & VFX for a couple of years and working on farms, I moved to Bergen when I was 22 and started to take some language classes and work in bars. Working in bars helped me to meet a wide variety of people and learn about what web development was through them. It also helped me a lot to develop my language skills and communicate well in Norwegian.

When I found out about the front-end development course with Noroff I was thrilled that I had finally found something that I felt I could really immerse myself in and enjoy.

I really enjoy being creative, but I am a logical thinker, so I feel very at home with front-end development. I get to build beautiful websites using code and problem solving.

I love cooking, reading, learning, photography, gaming, animals, trying new things and all things fantasy. I like technical finicky little things which currently has my other half teaching me how to play golf in my spare time. It's not just hitting a ball like I first thought. In fact, it's really hard to hit the ball at all when you start!

When I'm not searching for the British spelling of colour in my CSS, you can usually find me out walking my dog and taking photos of him, finishing a puzzle that's taken up all the space on my dining table or watching Lord of the Rings again.

Through my studies at Noroff I have learnt how to use a variety of technology and gained a lot of front-end development skills. Some of the technology I have learnt to use include Adobe XD, Adobe Photoshop and Adobe Illustrator for designing and JavaScript, React, Bootstrap, SASS, WordPress API and Strapi API for developing websites.
